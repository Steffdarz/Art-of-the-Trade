Sunday, April 6, 2025

This Week's Market Summary

GM Lads, How is everyone enjoying the bear market so far? Jk, I don’t even see things as a bull or bear market anymore now, the market shifts, there’s only short term selling opportunity, short term buying opportunity, and I’ll abide by it. We've seen NASDAQ, S&P, and some of the MAG 7 sell off, with the dollar surging – all because of the Trump tariffs, which seems to be the main sticking point. Global markets ended the week under pressure with a level of heightened volatility. If you look at the VIX, if you look at the move index, this is all causing a level of rising macro uncertainty. There are multiple risks converging as we head into next week.

Objectively, markets perform well when the level of uncertainty is relatively low, but when you have a wide probable array of uncertainties, it causes a level of uneasiness in the market. There are too many moving parts to even think about a sustained rally. Yes, you're going to get mechanical rotations and oscillations – I'm not saying we're just going to go straight down from here.

Bitcoin So Far

Bitcoin Weekly Chart with Key Support/Resistance Levels and Market Structure
No Bueno

Looking at Bitcoin, we've seen a phase of consolidation. The 12, 20, and 25 weekly EMAs are acting as a level of resistance, along with the 12-25s on the daily. Any moves above the 12 and 25 have rejected, and once we trade through the level, we see expansion to the downside. The key here is any acceptance below this low is likely to lead to expansion lower, which we saw back in 2021.

Fast-forward to today, it's a very similar structure: trend down, consolidation – are we going to see continuation into next week? When you look at the bands, they're in a very similar position as to what we saw in 2021. Price is being capped by the bands. There's likely to be a shift in context until proven otherwise.

Currently Bitcoin is at the lower bound of its range (77K-82.5K). Given the level of uncertainty and the number of times the 81-82K level has been tested, I'm not looking to rush into a long just yet. This level could act as resistance until proven otherwise. If we see acceptance below the current range, we could potentially trade into the 74-76K zone.

The dollar surged late on Friday, which suggests growing demand for dollar liquidity as people start unwinding out of their risk-on positions. What further added fuel to the fire was Powell's comments in his speech, emphasizing patience in response to inflation uncertainty, but the markets viewed his tone as out of touch given the rising volatility.

The charts to watch are very simple: first, track the bond volatility with the move index, then look at the VIX – any further spikes will cause more selling in risk-on markets. Watch for dollar to rally later in the week, watch for bonds to rally and yields to drop off. Look at commodities as well – gold, silver, copper have already fallen off a cliff over this last week.

Macro Analysis

Multiple macro factors are converging to create heightened uncertainty:

  • Trump Tariffs: The main sticking point driving market volatility
  • US-China Trade Tensions: Escalating tensions adding to global uncertainty
  • European Digital Tariffs: Potential targeting of US tech giants including MAX 7 companies
  • FOMC Meeting: Scheduled for Wednesday, adding to market unpredictability
  • CPI Data: Coming Thursday, further complicating the week ahead

These macro factors collectively suggest we may see continued volatility before any clear direction is established. Markets traditionally perform poorly during periods of high uncertainty, which is what we're experiencing now.

Market Outlook and Trading Opportunities

In terms of the calendar, we also have a level of uncertainty because on Wednesday we've got the FOMC meeting, and on Thursday we have CPI data. All in all, we have a lot of data points, so you really have to navigate this week very carefully.

Trading Strategy:

  • Keep trades very short-term; avoid swing positions heading into FOMC and CPI
  • If looking for longs, watch for acceptance back inside the range with stops at the lows
  • For potential shorts, look for rejection at the 82.5K resistance level
  • Reduce risk significantly due to expected volatility
  • Be very selective and avoid overtrading in this uncertain environment

If you don't have experience trading these environments, I'd advise you to just observe, maybe take some simulation trades. See how you get on, collect some data, get familiar with trading these volatile environments. Play safe – you're not going to hit the big trade in this condition with so many risk factors flying around.
