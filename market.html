Week of April 6, 2025

This Week's Market Summary

Bitcoin remains in a bullish trend after consolidating around the $85,000 level following its new all-time high of $87,450 set on March 28, 2025. Despite some intraweek volatility triggered by options expiry, BTC has maintained its higher low structure and continues to show resilience above the key $82,000 support zone.

The supply shock narrative continues to dominate market psychology, with on-chain metrics showing accelerated accumulation from long-term holders.

Bitcoin Price Analysis

Bitcoin Daily Chart with Key Support/Resistance Levels and Market Structure

After setting a new all-time high at $87,450, Bitcoin has entered a period of consolidation, forming a bull flag pattern on the daily timeframe. This pattern typically signals continuation rather than reversal, suggesting we may see another leg up if the market breaks above the upper trendline around $86,000.

Volume profile indicates strong support has formed in the $82,000-$83,000 range, where significant buying pressure emerged during last week's retracement. This zone coincides with the 0.382 Fibonacci retracement level of the recent rally, further strengthening its significance as support.

The 20-day EMA continues to provide dynamic support and has been rising steadily, currently at approximately $81,500. As long as price maintains above this level, the short-term bullish structure remains intact.

The technical picture remains predominantly bullish, with price maintaining above all major moving averages. The MACD has recently formed a bullish crossover, suggesting momentum may be returning after the consolidation phase. RSI at 58 indicates room for further upside before reaching overbought territory.

However, declining volume during the consolidation period is something to monitor, as significant price advances typically require increasing volume for sustainability. The narrowing Bollinger Bands suggest a volatility expansion may be imminent, potentially coinciding with a decisive move out of the current range.

On-Chain Analysis

On-chain metrics continue to signal a supply-constrained environment following the recent halving:

  • Exchange Reserves: Bitcoin on exchanges has decreased by 0.8% this week, continuing the outflow trend as coins move to cold storage
  • Illiquid Supply: The percentage of supply classified as illiquid reached 78.4%, an all-time high
  • Realized Price: Currently at $39,850, significantly below market price, indicating strong holder conviction
  • MVRV Ratio: At 2.12, above the historical average but below peak bull market levels above 3.0
  • Miner Selling Pressure: Decreased by 15% this week as more efficient operations maintain profitability despite the reward reduction

These on-chain indicators collectively suggest we are still in the early-to-mid phase of this bull cycle, with potential for further appreciation as supply continues to contract against rising demand.

Market Outlook and Trading Opportunities

The current consolidation pattern provides trading opportunities in both directions, though the primary trend remains bullish:

Bullish Scenario:

  • A decisive break above $86,000 with increasing volume would confirm bull flag completion
  • Target areas include $90,000 (psychological level) and $92,500 (Fibonacci extension)
  • Look for RSI to move above 60 to confirm momentum shift

Bearish Scenario:

  • Break below $82,000 support would signal potential deeper correction
  • Watch for possible test of the 50-day EMA around $77,500
  • Further weakness could target the $75,000 level, which represents the 0.618 Fibonacci retracement

Given the recent consolidation after a strong rally, risk management is crucial. Consider reduced position sizes and clear invalidation levels for any new trades. The high timeframe trend remains bullish, suggesting alignment with the primary trend offers the highest probability setups.
